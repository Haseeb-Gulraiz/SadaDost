Your decisions (what the brief asks you to defend)

Point at the amber node, then read the card.
1 No approved answer / out of scope
"What if the answer isn't in the approved content at all?"
Two buckets: out-of-scope (crypto) → plainly decline, no false human promise; never invent policy, fees or steps.
2 Problem seen, no policy
"What if it sees a problem in the data but has no approved policy?"
Acknowledge what's visible, then escalate to a human (e.g. duplicate Netflix charge, restricted account).
3 Data that must not be shared
"What if a customer asks for info that shouldn't be shared?"
Three layers: restricted never enters the prompt · guardrail PII check · regex floor scrubs the output.
4 Answer vs. escalate
"How does it tell 'I can answer' from 'a human should take this'?"
A binary grounding check: the model says whether approved content fully answers it — if not, it offers a human (no brittle score to wobble).
5 Ambiguity & language
"Where materials are ambiguous or two-way — decide and note it."
Physical-vs-virtual card unknown → state the condition, don't guess. Reply in the customer's language, else English.

grounding · safety · escalation are all deterministic code…
…the LLM only phrases. So the safety guarantees hold even if the model misbehaves.
